Sickle Cell Disease: Unmet Medical Need

Disease Background

  • Single nucleotide mutation in β-globin gene (HBB)
  • Polymerization of deoxygenated sickle hemoglobin (HbS)
  • Vaso-occlusive crises, chronic hemolysis, organ damage
  • ~100,000 patients in US; millions worldwide

Molecular Biology of Hemoglobin Switching and Hemoglobin Types

Therapeutic Strategy

Reactivation of fetal hemoglobin (HbF) in adult red blood cells provides disease-modifying treatment for SCD.

Assay Strategy & Design Rationale

Tier 1: High-Throughput Biochemical Screening TR-FRET Assay, LC/MS ect.

Tier 2: Target Engagement in vitro alphaLISA in immortalized cell line, limited treatment time

  • ⭐ Tier 3: Physiological Relevant Phenotypic Confirmation ⭐
    • Primary cell culture model
    • Nanostring gene expression
    • alphaLISA protein quantification
    • Built for screening ≈ 20 compounds weekly

Tier 4: Candidate Selection 3-5 candidates, in vivo testing

Assay Endpoints & Data Capture

Nanostring nCounter Gene Expression

Primary Endpoints: - HBG1/HBG2: Fetal γ-globin mRNA - HBB: Adult β-globin expression - γ/(γ+β) Ratio: Hemoglobin switching

Mechanistic Insights: - BCL11A, KLF1, MYB: Transcriptional regulators - GATA1, TAL1, EPOR: Erythroid markers

Data Output: - Digital counts, normalized to housekeeping genes - Fold-change vs vehicle control

EnVision alphaLISA Protein Quantification

Primary Endpoints: - HbF Protein: Direct fetal hemoglobin measurement - Total Hemoglobin: Cell health/differentiation - %HbF: Clinical translational endpoint

Advantages: - High sensitivity (pg/mL range) - No compound interference, 384-well compatible

Data Output: - Absolute protein concentration, EC₅₀ determination - mRNA-protein correlation analysis

Dual-platform approach: mRNA mechanistic insights + clinically relevant protein endpoints

Reflections and Learnings

Challenges & Solutions

Technical Challenges

1. Donor Variability: 3-5 fold baseline HbF differences - Solution: Normalized metrics; qualified donor panel

2. Culture Reproducibility: Batch-to-batch variation - Solution: Lot testing; controlled serum batches

3. Throughput Limitations: Manual culture steps - Solution: Semi-automation; batch processing

Analytical Challenges

4. Nanostring Throughput: 12-24 samples/run limitation - Solution: Prioritized compounds; alphaLISA primary

5. Data Integration: Correlating mRNA and protein - Solution: Custom pipeline; unified database

6. Assay Interference: Autofluorescence from compounds - Solution: AlphaLISA format immune to interference

Supplementary Data & Technical Details

Nanostring nCounter Background

Technology Overview

Digital molecular barcoding for direct mRNA quantification without amplification

Custom CodeSet Design

  • Target Genes: HBG1, HBG2 (γ-globin), HBB, HBA1/2
  • Hemoglobin Switching: BCL11A, KLF1, MYB, SOX6
  • Erythroid Markers: GATA1, TAL1, EPOR
  • Housekeeping: GAPDH, ACTB, HPRT1
  • Total: 15-20 genes per panel

Workflow Implementation

  1. Cell lysis & mRNA isolation
  2. Hybridization with probe sets (16-18 hrs)
  3. Nanostring nCounter detection
  4. Digital counting & normalization
  5. Data analysis pipeline

Throughput: 12-24 samples per run

Turnaround: 48 hours

Key Advantage: Minimal sample input (100 ng RNA)
