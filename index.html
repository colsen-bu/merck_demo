Sickle Cell Disease: Unmet Medical Need

Disease Background

  • Single nucleotide mutation in β-globin gene (HBB)
  • Polymerization of deoxygenated sickle hemoglobin (HbS)
  • Vaso-occlusive crises, chronic hemolysis, organ damage
  • ~100,000 patients in US; millions worldwide

Assay Strategy & Design Rationale

Tier 1: High-Throughput Biochemical Screening TR-FRET Assay, LC/MS ect.

Tier 2: Target Engagement in vitro alphaLISA in immortalized cell line, limited treatment time

  • ⭐ Tier 3: Physiological Relevant Phenotypic Confirmation ⭐
    • Primary cell culture model
    • Nanostring gene expression
    • alphaLISA protein quantification
    • Built for screening ≈ 20 compounds weekly

Tier 4: Candidate Selection 3-5 candidates, in vivo testing

Reflections and Learnings

Challenges & Solutions

Technical Challenges

1. Donor Variability: Baseline HbF differences. - Solution: Screen multiple donors ahead of production to locate ideal profile.

2. Culture Reproducibility: Batch-to-batch variation - Solution: Automation enables consistent replating at consistent culture densisties.

3. Throughput Limitations: Manual culture steps. - Solution: Cell Culture Semi-automation.

Analytical Challenges

4. Nanostring Analysis: nSolver software used for discovery not built for screening. - Solution: Deconvoluted nSolver processing and implemented in Python.

5. Data Integration: Correlating mRNA and protein - Solution: Collected both Nanostring and alphaLISA data

6. Assay Interference: Concerns over plate Edge effects. - Solution: Throughput improvements allow for testing compounds multiple times

Supplementary Data & Technical Details

Nanostring nCounter: Laboratory Procedure & Custom Assay Development

Standard Laboratory Workflow

Day 1: Sample Preparation & Hybridization 1. Cell lysis in manufacturer’s buffer (5-10 min) 2. mRNA isolation (optional - can use crude lysate) 3. Mix 5 µL sample (100 ng RNA) with probe cocktail 4. Hybridize overnight at 65°C (16-18 hours)

Day 2: Automated Processing 1. Load hybridized samples into nCounter Prep Station 2. Automated washing, immobilization, and alignment (~3 hours) 3. Load cartridge into nCounter Digital Analyzer 4. Digital counting of molecular barcodes (~6 hours)

Day 3: Data Analysis - Export raw counts - Normalize to housekeeping genes - Calculate fold-change vs controls

Key Insight: Custom Assay Development

Building a custom Nanostring assay is primarily a bioinformatics exercise, not molecular biology:

  • Probe Design: Computational selection of optimal binding regions
  • Specificity Analysis: In silico validation against transcriptome
  • Cross-reactivity Testing: Sequence alignment to avoid off-targets

Unlike PCR/qPCR, no primers to optimize, no cycling conditions to test

Custom alphaLISA Development: From Antibody to Assay

Development Workflow

Phase 1: Antibody Selection (2-4 weeks) - Screen commercial antibodies for HbF specificity - Test antibody pairs for sandwich compatibility - Validate no cross-reactivity with HbA/HbS

Phase 2: Conjugation & Optimization (3-4 weeks) - Conjugate capture Ab to acceptor beads - Biotinylate detection Ab for donor beads - Optimize bead ratios and incubation times - Test buffer conditions

Phase 3: Validation (2-3 weeks) - Generate standard curve with purified HbF - Determine dynamic range (LOD, LLOQ, ULOQ) - Test matrix interference and precision (CV%)

Timeline: 7-11 weeks | Cost: ~$30K-45K

Key Principle: Proximity-Based Detection

AlphaLISA requires antibody pair binding within 200 nm for singlet oxygen energy transfer

Critical: No cross-reactivity with adult hemoglobin (HbA/HbS) essential for SCD assay

alphaLISA Advantages

✓ No wash steps - homogeneous format
✓ Low sample - 5 µL lysate/well
✓ Wide dynamic range - 4-5 logs
✓ Matrix tolerant - crude lysate OK
✓ High sensitivity

Common Challenges

• Hook Effect: High [HbF] saturates beads → signal drop
• Matrix Effects: Lysate components quench signal
• Antibody Selection: Most time-consuming step
